Semi Conductors

The different types of semi conductors and how they function

Date: July 11, 2024 | Author: Vanuja Ranawana

A semi conductor is a material that has more electrical resistance than conductors and less than insulators hence it can conduct more current than an insulator but less than a conductor.

Electrons within any material exists in distinct energy levels however within a semiconductor there are two bands. The conduction and the valence band. All the electrons are present within the valence band however if we increase the temperate and input energy into the system electrons will transfer form the valence to the conduction band.

This causes the conduction of current in both bands das the electrons within the conduction band moves while the electrons within the valence band also move to fill the newly created hole, this means that the holes move causing the conduction of electricity. This type of semi conductor is known as an Intrinsic semiconductor.

N-type semi conductors are a different type of conductor where impurity atoms are placed carefully between the conductor and valence band. These atoms have an extra electron and have the same energy level as the conduction band.

Then conduction only occurs within the conduction band due to the flow of atoms. Conduction does not occur in the valence band since the electrons are packed and cannot flow. (N-types semi conductors are termed as such due to electrons being usually represented by the letter n).

The last of the 3 semiconductors is called p-type semiconductor. Within the semiconductor the impurity atoms are placed near the valence band hence causing them to accept the electrons from the band which therefore leaves behind holes. Hence the conduction of electricity occurs due to the hole movement within the valence band. The p in p-type refers to protons since holes are considered to be positively charged.

LED's are constructed from semiconductors. LED stands for light emitting diode and are made from an n type and p type materials. This then forms an n-p type junction / energy barrier which prevents the electrons and holes from moving over the junction.

However if the voltage applied across the diode is increase it would compel the electrons to move over the energy barrier there for reducing it.

Eventually when the voltage is high enough the electrons and holes begin to flow, this causes a process known as electron hole pair recombination to occur. The electrons form the high energy level moves down to the lower hole's energy level. This energy is then given out as a photon.The energy of the photon is equal to the energy gap. Hence different wavelengths of light can be formed based on the energy barrier.

LED's only function when current flow in one direction. If the direction of current was changed the energy barrier will increase hence further preventing the flow of electrons and holes.
